César Franck International Piano Competition

14th Edition | 18-20 December 2026

Honoring the legacy of César Franck. Providing an international stage for the next generation of musical talent in Brussels.

Onze Missie

Artistieke Excellentie & Educatie

De CFIPC-missie, opgericht in 2013 door de Belgische pianist Philippe Raskin, is om nieuwe generaties jonge pianisten aan te moedigen hun eigen mogelijkheden te overstijgen door een internationaal platform te bieden.

Met 70 deelnemers uit meer dan 25 verschillende landen en een internationaal geprezen jury in 2024, heeft de César Franck zich al gevestigd als een toonaangevende wedstrijd voor jonge muzikanten wereldwijd.

De integratie van de wedstrijd, concerten en het educatieprogramma als een jaarlijks evenement symboliseert onze toewijding en passie voor de bevordering van cultuur en onderwijs.

Programma-eisen

1. Repertoirekeuze

Het programma is vrij te kiezen voor categorie A en B. Categorie C en D moeten een werk uit het Weense Classicisme toevoegen; de rest is vrij.

Categorieën C & D moeten ten minste één deel van een "Weense Klassieke" componist (Haydn, Mozart of Beethoven) opnemen in hun finale-programma.

2. Het is niet verplicht maar aanbevolen om ten minste één werk van een Belgische componist op te nemen in de halve finale of de finale.

3. Uitvoering uit het hoofd is verplicht voor beide rondes van de wedstrijd.

4. Het is NIET mogelijk om het halve finale-programma na indiening te wijzigen.

5. Kandidaten mogen in hun finale-programma eerder opgenomen werken uit de eerste ronde herhalen.

6. Finalisten moeten hun partituren voor 15 november 2026 gescand opsturen naar office@cfipc.com.

Halve Finale Tijdslimiet

  • Categorie A: 6 min
  • Categorie B: 8 min
  • Categorie C: 10 min
  • Categorie D: 15 min

Finale Tijdslimiet

  • Categorie A: 10 min
  • Categorie B: 12 min
  • Categorie C: 15 min
  • Categorie D: 30 min

Halve Finales (Online)

  • 1. Video-inzendingen mogen NIET bewerkt zijn (audio).
  • 2. Alle opnames moeten in 2026 zijn gemaakt in één take zonder montages.
  • 3. Uploaden naar YouTube, Vimeo of Dropbox.
  • 4. Halve finale resultaten worden op 10 november 2026 bekendgemaakt.
  • 5. Maximaal 5 finalisten per categorie.

Algemene Reglementen

1. De wedstrijd staat open voor pianisten van alle nationaliteiten, 21 jaar of jonger op het moment van de finales (18-20 december 2026).

2. Kandidaten moeten een CV, hoge resolutie foto en kopie van het paspoort indienen.

3. Inschrijving is pas definitief na ontvangst van het inschrijfgeld.

4. De beslissing van de jury is bindend en onherroepelijk.

5. De organisatie behoudt zich het recht voor om opnames te maken voor archief- en promotiedoeleinden.

6. Minderjarigen moeten te allen tijde worden begeleid door een volwassene.

Our Mission

Artistic Excellence & Education

Established in 2013 by Belgian pianist Philippe Raskin, the CFIPC mission is to encourage new generations of young pianists to transcend their own capabilities by providing an international platform.

With 70 participants originating from more than 25 different countries and an internationally acclaimed jury in 2024, the César Franck has already established itself as a leading competition for young musicians worldwide.

The incorporation of the competition, concerts and education program as a yearly event symbolizes our commitment and passion to the advancement of culture and education.

Program

1. For both rounds of the competition, a free program can be chosen for Category A & B applicants however applicants from Categories C & D must include at least one movement from a “Viennese Classic” composer (Haydn, Mozart, or Beethoven) in their Finals program.

2. It is not obligatory but recommended to include at least one piece written by a Belgian composer in the semi-final or the final round. A list of Belgian composers can be found here.

3. Memory interpretation is required for both rounds of the competition except for certain contemporary pieces after a personal request and approval by the competition.

4. It is NOT possible to change the semi-finals program after submission. For all selected finalists, it is possible to change your finals program until 15th November 2026.

5. Candidates may include in their Finals program previously recorded works from the 1st round.

6. A copy of your program (Urtext or original editions as preferred by the jury) must be scanned and sent by all finalists to office@cfipc.com for the jury before 15th of November 2026. All finalists must submit the scores of their entire programme by the official deadline (November 15th). Programme changes are not permitted after this date. Any candidate who fails to submit the required scores by the deadline risks disqualification.

Semi-Finals Limits

  • Cat A: Max 6 min
  • Cat B: Max 8 min
  • Cat C: Max 10 min
  • Cat D: Max 15 min

Finals Limits

  • Cat A: Max 10 min
  • Cat B: Max 12 min
  • Cat C: Max 15 min
  • Cat D: Max 30 min

Semi-Finals (Online)

  • 1. All video submissions should not be edited (audio). All edited videos will be automatically disqualified.
  • 2. All recordings submitted by candidates must be made in 2026 and recorded in one take without cuts, also between pieces, like in a live competition (i.e. a single link).
  • 3. All video submissions must be uploaded directly to an online service where the video can be streamed online without downloading (YouTube, Vimeo, Dropbox, etc). No WeTransfer or DVDs.
  • 4. Candidates must check if their link is online and viewable after submission until announcement of finalists.
  • 5. Candidates must ensure video quality for impartial judging. Upright pianos and smartphones are accepted and will not be penalised.
  • 6. A maximum of 5 finalists will be selected (subject to jury discretion).
  • 7. Results announced on 10th of November 2026 via email and social media.

General Rules

1. The 14th César Franck International Piano Competition is open to pianists of all nationalities aged 21 or less at the time of the competition Finals 18th, 19th & 20th of December 2026 (opening concert on December 18th). The finals will take place live at De Lijsterbes (Kraainem, Belgium).

2. Applicants must complete the online application form and upload: CV, high-res digital photo, passport scan (candidate & parent if under 18), proof of payment, complete program for both rounds with timings, and the video submission link.

3. Your candidacy will only be confirmed after we have received the application fee.

4. A maximum of 5 participants from each category will be selected to perform in the finals (jury discretion).

5. The decision of the jury is final and will not be subjected to reviews or appeals.

6. Results of semi-finals announced on 10th November 2026.

7. The application fee is not refundable; candidates responsible for bank/PayPal fees.

8. Entry in the competition implies acceptance of present rules without reservation.

9. Registration acts as an agreement for all candidates and parents.

10. Organizers reserve the right to record all performances for broadcast and archival use. Candidates renounce all rights and claims regarding recordings.

11. Contestants under 18 must be accompanied by an adult carrying full responsibility at all times.

12. All travel expenses (tickets, visas) are carried by the participant. Invitation letters provided upon request.

13. Contestants responsible for accommodation costs (special hotel deal will be offered).

14. Competition not liable for accidents/incidents beyond standard venue insurance.

15. Advised to check personal insurance for accidents outside the competition venue.

16. Teacher/professor names will not be printed in the jury program.

17. Jury members will not vote for their own students.

18. Rules may be subject to change without notice.

The Legacy

Founded in 2013 by Belgian pianist Philippe Raskin, the CFIPC was born from a desire to encourage young pianists to transcend their own capabilities.

Located in the heart of Europe, the competition has welcomed participants from over 30 countries, establishing itself as a premier destination for piano education and excellence.
