César Franck International Piano Competition

14th Edition | 18-20 December 2026

Honoring the legacy of César Franck. Providing an international stage for the next generation of musical talent in Brussels.

Our Mission

Artistic Excellence & Education

Established in 2013 by Belgian pianist Philippe Raskin, the CFIPC mission is to encourage new generations of young pianists to transcend their own capabilities by providing an international platform.

With 70 participants originating from more than 25 different countries and an internationally acclaimed jury in 2024, the César Franck has already established itself as a leading competition for young musicians worldwide.

The incorporation of the competition, concerts and education program as a yearly event symbolizes our commitment and passion to the advancement of culture and education.

Program Requirements

1

Free program for Categories A & B. Categories C & D must include at least one movement from a "Viennese Classic" composer (Haydn, Mozart, or Beethoven) in their Finals program.

2

It is recommended (but not obligatory) to include at least one piece by a Belgian composer in either round.

3

Memory interpretation is required for both rounds, except for certain contemporary pieces (approval required).

4

Semi-final programs cannot be changed after submission. Finals programs may be updated until 15th November 2026.

5

Finalists must scan and send their scores (Urtext/Original) to office@cfipc.com before 15th November. Failure to submit risks disqualification.

Semi-Finals (Online)

  • • No audio editing permitted; edited videos will be disqualified.
  • • Recordings must be made in 2026 in one single take without cuts.
  • • Upload to YouTube, Vimeo, Dropbox, etc. (No WeTransfer/DVD).
  • • Upright pianos and smartphone recordings are accepted and will not be penalized.
  • • A maximum of 5 finalists per category will be selected.
  • • Results announced on 10th November 2026.

Special Performance Opportunities

1st Prize winners from each category will have the opportunity to play with orchestra in April 2027. Additionally, a concert opportunity in Milano will be offered to one winner by Vincenzo Balzani.

General Regulations

1. Open to pianists of all nationalities aged 21 or less at the time of the Finals (Dec 18-20, 2026).

2. Application requires: CV, high-res photo, passport scan, supervising adult scan (if under 18), proof of payment, and program details.

3. Candidacy confirmed only after application fee receipt.

4. Jury decisions are final and not subject to reviews or appeals.

5. Organizers reserve the right to record all performances for broadcast and archival purposes.

6. Minors must be accompanied by a responsible adult at all times during the competition period.

7. Travel and accommodation costs are the responsibility of the participants.

8. The competition venue is Community Centre De Lijsterbes. Participants are advised to check personal insurance coverage.

9. Teacher names are not printed in the jury program; jury members do not vote for their own students.

10. Rules may be subject to change without notice.

The Legacy

Founded in 2013 by Belgian pianist Philippe Raskin, the CFIPC was born from a desire to encourage young pianists to transcend their own capabilities.

Located in the heart of Europe, the competition has welcomed participants from over 30 countries, establishing itself as a premier destination for piano education and excellence.

"Music is the expression of the movement of the soul. We listen to that movement in every artist who performs with us."

— President's Note

Program & Repertoire

Viennese Classics: Categories C & D must include at least one movement by Haydn, Mozart, or Beethoven in their Finals program.

Free Choice: Categories A & B may choose a completely free program for both rounds.

Belgian Heritage: It is highly recommended to include at least one piece by a Belgian composer.

Performance: Memory interpretation is required for all pieces except specific contemporary works (upon request).

Online Semi-Finals

Integrity: Video submissions must be recorded in one take without any audio editing or cuts.

Recent Material: All recordings must be made in 2026 specifically for this competition.

Submission: Links must be streamable (YouTube, Vimeo, etc.). WeTransfer or physical DVDs are not accepted.

Selection: A maximum of 5 finalists per category will be invited to the live round in Brussels.
