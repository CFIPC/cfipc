Program Requirements

1. Repertoire Choice

Program is free of choice for Category A and B.

Category C and D must include at least one movement from a “Viennese Classic” composer (Haydn, Mozart, or Beethoven) in their Finals program. The rest is free choice.

2. It is not obligatory but recommended to include at least one piece written by a Belgian composer in the semi-final or the final round. A list of Belgian composers can be found here.

3. Memory interpretation is required for both rounds of the competition except for certain contemporary pieces after a personal request and approval by the competition.

4. It is NOT possible to change the semi-finals program after submission. For all selected finalists, it is possible to change your finals program until 15th November 2026.

5. Candidates may include in their Finals program previously recorded works from the 1st round.

6. A copy of your program (Urtext or original editions as preferred by the jury) must be scanned and sent by all finalists to office@cfipc.com for the jury before 15th of November 2026. All finalists must submit the scores of their entire programme by the official deadline (November 15th). Programme changes are not permitted after this date. Any candidate who fails to submit the required scores by the deadline risks disqualification.

Semi-Finals (Online)

  • Technical 1 All video submissions should not be edited (audio). All edited videos will be automatically disqualified.
  • Technical 2 All recordings submitted by candidates must be made in 2026 and recorded in one take without cuts, also between pieces, like in a live competition.
  • Technical 3 All video submissions must be uploaded directly to an online service (YouTube, Vimeo, Dailymotion, Dropbox). No WeTransfer or DVDs.
  • Verification It is the responsibility of the candidates to check if their link is online and viewable after submission until finalists are announced.
  • Quality Smartphone/Upright piano recordings are accepted and will not be penalised. Candidates must ensure impartial judging via quality.
  • Selection A maximum of 5 finalists will be selected (subject to jury discretion).
  • Results Results announced on 10th of November 2026 via email and social media.

Scores & Finals Program

1. The 14th César Franck International Piano Competition is open to pianists of all nationalities aged 21 or less at the time of the competition Finals 18th, 19th & 20th of December 2026. The finals will take place live at De Lijsterbes, Kraainem, Belgium.

2. Applicants must upload: CV, high-res photo (max 2MB), candidate/parent passport scan, proof of payment, program with timings, and video link no later than 1st November 2026.

3. Your candidacy will only be confirmed after we have received the application fee.

4. A maximum of 5 participants from each category will be selected to perform in the finals.

5. The decision of the jury is final and will not be subjected to reviews or appeals.

6. The entry in the competition implies the acceptance of the present rules without any reservation. Registration acts as an agreement for all candidates and their parents.

7. Organizers reserve the right to record all performances. Candidates renounce all rights, fees and claims regarding such recordings and broadcasts.

8. All contestants under the age of 18 must be accompanied by an adult carrying full responsibility at all times.

9. All travel expenses are to be carried out by the participant. Invitation letters provided upon request.

10. Contestants are responsible for their own accommodation costs. Special hotel deals will be offered to accepted candidates.

11. The competition will not be held liable for accidents beyond standard visitor insurance of the venue. Advised to check personal insurance of contestants.

12. The name of the candidate’s teacher/professor will not be printed in the program for the jury. Jury members will not vote for their own students.

Full Regulations 2026: Application fees are non-refundable. Rules may be subject to change without notice. Failure to submit required scores by Nov 15th risks disqualification.
